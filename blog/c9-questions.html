Asking Good Questions

How to Get a Good Response

12/4/14

Getting great answers starts with great questions. Keep these tips in mind when formulating a question:

  • Start with a descriptive subject/title.

    • It's better to give a concise yet descriptive title than a short title that refers to your issue. If the reader has a clear idea of what your problem is from the subject you're more likely to get people to read through your issue. If your subject is too broad or not pointed, people will most likely scroll right past your question.

  • Give a brief background on your problem.

    • After you've given a clear subject/title give your reader some background on your problem. Include what you understand and where you're having problems.

  • Share where you're at.

    • Paste in your code or a screenshot if applicable. This will catch your reader up to where you are stuck.

  • Watch your formatting, DON'T YELL.

    • Internet boards, forums, and social media are funny places. Tempers flare over, well, pretty much everything and nothing at the same time. Remember to keep your internet etiquette checked or face the wrath of never ending off-topic arguments.
